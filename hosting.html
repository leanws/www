When free hosting is not really free

There are many websites advertising "free hosting" on Google, but often their free service has significant restrictions inappropriate for a business website, and customers have to pay to get rid of those.

Here are some examples:

  • www.wix.com: free only for subdomains like name.wixsite.com with ads on your website.
  • Free Domain & Reliable Hosting | web.com‎: hosting is actually not free there.
  • freehosting.com - host without cost: no free SSL.
  • WordPress: free only for subdomains like name.wordpress.com with ads on your website.
A European company freehosting.com would be the only (more or less) acceptable solution out of this list. However, due to the absence of SSL, the website hosted there will be penalized in Google search. Lean Web Solutions has a reduced functionality mirror hosted on freehosting.com. Still, we are not considering freehosting.com as a primary hosting platform because
  • there are online reviews reporting unreliability of freehosting.com: "You might not gain access to your domain name for days and you can’t transfer it even once you get it. The pages load incredibly slow compared with paid web hosting plans, and the site goes down quite a bit" and
  • with freehosting.com customers have to pay for the things that they receive for free from other hosters: SSL certificate, databases, subdomains, etc.
